Since I came to Iasi, me and my friend (who is also pretty passionate about amateur radio) really wanted to check out airband traffic, because we live relatively close to the Iasi Airport.

But first of all, let me make a quick introduction to amateur radio and all the shenanigans related to it.

What is Amateur Radio?

To directly quote Wikipedia, "Amateur radio, also known as ham radio, is the use of the radio frequency spectrum for purposes of non-commercial exchange of messages, wireless experimentation, self-training, private recreation, radiosport, contesting, and emergency communications."

Because the radio space is regulated, to be able to transmit, one needs a amateur radio license, just like a driving license. To get such license, one needs to take a certain exam for the category they wish to use; each category allowing the owner of the license to transmit at different power rates.

Another cool thing about amateur radio communications is the fact that each amater radio operator (also called "ham radio operator") gets a callsign that is used to identify the operator. Each operator is required by law to announce their callsign at the beginning of each transmission.

Then what is airband?
